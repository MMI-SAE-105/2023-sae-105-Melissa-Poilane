Superkilen

Superkilen, a park inaugurated in 2012 in Nørrebro, is more than just a green space. green space: it's a collective work of art reflecting the cultural diversity of Copenhagen.

Multiculturalism at the Superkilen Center

One of Superkilen's most striking features is its cultural diversity, displayed diversity. Divided into three distinct sections - the Red Square, the Black Square and the Green Garden - the park is home to of elements from over fifty countries, offering an immersive and inclusive experience.

An Artistic and Architectural Patchwork

Each element of Superkilen is carefully selected to represent the cultural identity of its region of origin. From Moroccan benches to Spanish fountains and Afghan swings, each corner of the park tells a unique story, creating a space where cultures meet.

A Place of Encounter and Celebration

Superkilen serves as a gathering place for locals and visitors alike, fostering intercultural exchange exchanges and offering a platform for celebrating diversity. Regular events regularly in the park reflect this desire to foster inclusion and dialogue between communities.
